Welcome to ViralBeat Studio

What is icosahedral symmetry?

Most viral capsids are built from identical protein subunits arranged with icosahedral symmetry — the same geometry as a soccer ball or a d20 die. This arrangement minimises the amount of genetic material needed to encode the shell while maximising its stability.

An icosahedron has 60 symmetry operations, expressed through three families of rotation axes:

  • 2-Fold axes (15 total) — pass through each of the 30 edges. Rotating 180° maps the capsid onto itself.
  • 3-Fold axes (10 total) — pass through each triangular face centre. Rotating 120° maps the capsid onto itself.
  • 5-Fold axes (6 total) — pass through each vertex. Rotating 72° maps the capsid onto itself.
